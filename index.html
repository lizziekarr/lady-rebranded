5 Overlooked Strategies for Recruiting New Business

Affirmation: I am open to pursuing new methods to making a connection with potential clients.

Fear of the unknown often holds us back in various avenues of our lives, and it certainly plays a role in a developing brand. At times, we are so afraid of failure that we limit ourselves by trying nothing new. But the greatest lessons are learned as we “fail forward,” try new methods and walk in courage.

1. The Incentive Referral

While many of us certainly ask our clients to refer our brands to their friends and colleagues we sometimes forget to incentivize the action. For example, by offering 15 percent off to the current client on future endeavors and 15-20% off to their referral, customers are even more eager to share your brand. By doing so, you not only show your appreciation to your client but also your value of continual relationship building beyond the first experience.

2. LinkedIn, Freelancer & More

We know that social media can already feel like a third job. But sites such as LinkedIn, Freelancer and Upwork allow brands to make a connection with potential clients that are actively seeking services. By setting up a profile and sending minor proposals we open up the door to clients who are currently (and openly) in need.
